...But why?

I can only respond with a simple meme that started it all:


This simple joke brought about in me, a machine learning and computer vision engineer, to a deeply philosophical and unnecessary question: "could a classifier be built for this?" From a computer vision perspective, the problem proved interesting because both classes were heavily biased towards the red channel in RGB colorspace. From a deep learning perspective, it felt to me like a rehash of the dog or muffin meme which I had also previously laughed about, but never touched. So on a particular snowy weekend of no particular importance in January, I found myself building the full stack of "Doom Or Lasagna". From training to classification, from backend Flask application to frontend HTML website, and finally from Docker image to EC2 instance, I ran through it all, for no other reason than just to say "this is what I can do in a weekend." I knew the resulting dumpster fire wasn't going to be pretty, and I knew at the end I wouldn't even have a useful capability or even a hackathon to submit it to. But somewhere along the way the project iself became a meme to me; a meme I couldn't let be a dream. To quote Cave Johnson: "Science isn't about Why, it's about Why Not."
